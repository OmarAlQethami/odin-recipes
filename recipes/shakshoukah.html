Shakshoukah

Shakshoukah is a Maghrebi dish of eggs poached in a sauce of tomatoes, olive oil, peppers, onion and garlic, commonly spiced with cumin, paprika and cayenne pepper. According to Joan Nathan, shakshouka originated in Ottoman North Africa in the mid-16th century after tomatoes were introduced to the region by Hernan Cort√©s as part of the Columbian exchange.

Ingredients:

  • Eggs
  • Oil
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Salt
  • Salsa
  • Spices

Steps:

  1. Pour the oil and cut some onion and tomato
  2. When the onion goes black crack the eggs into the platter
  3. Put the salt, Salsa and spices
  4. Move it left and right for 10 minutes
  5. Happy eating!
